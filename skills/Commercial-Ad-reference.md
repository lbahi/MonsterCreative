# 1 Cinematic Shot & Movement Reference

## Description

A comprehensive reference guide for cinematic shot types, camera angles, and movement techniques. Use this skill to craft precise, film-literate prompts for AI image and video generation. Covers everything from basic framing (EWS to Macro) to advanced camera movements (Dolly Zoom, Bullet Time, FPV Drone).

## When to Use

- Writing prompts for AI image generators (Midjourney, DALL-E, Stable Diffusion, etc.)
- Writing prompts for AI video generators (Runway, Pika, Sora, etc.)
- Storyboarding or pre-visualization
- Any time you need to specify camera framing, angle, or motion with professional precision

## How to Use

1. **Identify the emotional or narrative goal** of your scene.
2. **Consult the reference tables** below to find the technique that matches your intent.
3. **Copy the example prompt** as a starting point, replacing `[img 1]` or subject descriptions with your own character/scene details.
4. **Combine techniques** (e.g., *Low Angle + Dolly In + Close-Up*) for more complex cinematic language.
5. **Add lighting, atmosphere, and style keywords** to complete the prompt.

## Part 1: Shots — Distance & Size

Use these to control how much of the subject and environment appears in frame.

| Shot | Framing | Purpose | Example Prompt |
|------|---------|---------|----------------|
| **Extreme Wide Shot (EWS)** | The subject is barely visible; emphasizes the vast landscape or location. | Establish the vastness, show isolation, or emphasize scale. | Extreme wide shot of [img 1] standing alone in a vast, empty frozen tundra. The subject is small in the distance, surrounded by massive snowy mountains. Cinematic lighting |
| **Wide Shot (WS) / Long Shot** | Shows the full subject (head to toe) and the environment. | Establish context, show subject's relationship to surroundings. | Wide shot of [img 1] standing full body in a snowstorm. She is wearing fur armor, boots visible, centered in a snowy forest clearing. Environmental context. |
| **Full Shot** | Frames the subject from head to toe, filling the frame roughly top to bottom. | Show full body language and costume/attire clearly. | Full body shot of [img 1] from head to toe. She stands confidently in the snow, holding a weapon at her side. Detailed costume design |
| **Medium Long Shot (MLS)** | Knees up. Also known as a "3/4 shot." | More focus on the subject while retaining some environment context. | Medium long shot of [img 1] framed from the knees up. She is walking through deep snow, looking towards the horizon. 3/4 angle view. |
| **Cowboy Shot** | Mid-thighs up. Originates from Westerns (to show the gun holster). | Powerful and confident framing, emphasizes action readiness. | Cowboy shot of [img 1] framed from mid-thigh up. Focus on her hands resting on her belt, confident stance, snowy background with soft bokeh. |
| **Medium Shot (MS)** | Waist up. The standard storytelling shot. | Captures body language and face, ideal for dialogue. | Medium shot of [img 1] from the waist up. She is looking directly at the camera with a serious expression. Snowflakes falling in the foreground. |
| **Medium Close-Up (MCU)** | Chest/Shoulders up. | Focuses on the face but keeps some distance for context. | Medium close-up of [img 1] framed from chest up. Focus on her face and fur collar. The wind is blowing her braided hair. Intimate and dramatic |
| **Close-Up (CU)** | Head and neck only. | Pure emotion and facial expression, high intimacy. | Close-up of [img 1], framing only her head and neck. Her piercing eyes looking into the lens. Frosty breath visible in the cold air. |
| **Extreme Close-Up (ECU)** | Focuses on a specific detail (just the eyes, a mouth, a finger pressing a button). | High intensity, highlights critical detail, pure focus. | Extreme close-up on the eyes of [img 1]. Macro detail of the iris, snowflakes caught in her eyelashes, war paint texture. Intense gaze |
| **Macro Shot** | Microscopic level detail (e.g., texture of fabric, a bug on a leaf). | Reveal texture or highly detailed, unseen elements. | Macro shot of the texture of [img 1]'s fur armor and the frost on her skin. Microscopic details of snow crystals and leather grain |

## Part 2: Shots — Angles

Use these to control the camera's vertical and rotational relationship to the subject.

| Angle | Description | Effect | Example Prompt |
|-------|-------------|--------|----------------|
| **Eye-Level** | Neutral perspective. | Realism; how we see the world naturally, creates connection. | Eye-level shot of [img 1] facing the camera. Neutral perspective, realistic documentary style portrait in the snow. |
| **Low Angle** | Camera looks **up** at the subject. | Makes them look powerful, dominant, or scary. | Low angle shot looking up at [img 1]. She towers over the camera, looking powerful and dominant against a stormy sky. |
| **High Angle** | Camera looks **down** at the subject. | Makes them look small, weak, or vulnerable. | High angle shot looking down at [img 1]. She is looking up towards the camera, snow falling onto her face. Vulnerable perspective. |
| **Overhead / Bird’s Eye View** | Looking straight down (90 degrees) from the sky. | Graphic and map-like, often used for scale or tracking. | Direct overhead bird's eye view of [img 1] standing in the snow. Top-down angle showing her shadow stretching on the white ground. |
| **Worm’s Eye View** | Extremely low, looking up from the ground level. | Exaggerates height/size, gives an unusual perspective. | Worm's eye view from the ground looking up at [img 1]'s boots and legs, with her face distant in the sky. Ant-man perspective |
| **Dutch Angle / Canted Angle** | The camera is tilted sideways (tilted horizon). | Creates unease, disorientation, or chaos. | Dutch angle shot of [img 1], tilted horizon line. Disorienting and chaotic atmosphere, battle ready pose, dynamic composition. |
| **Over-the-Shoulder (OTS)** | Looking past one person’s shoulder at another. | Essential for dialogue, grounds the conversation. | Over-the-shoulder shot looking past a blurry Viking warrior in the foreground, focusing on [img 1] who is staring back at them. |
| **Point of View (POV)** | The camera *is* the character’s eyes. | Viewer experiences what the character sees, high immersion. | First person POV shot of someone approaching [img 1]. She is looking directly at the viewer, hand extended as if greeting the camera. |
| **Selfie Angle** | High angle, arm’s length, facing the subject. | Common in social media aesthetics, informal, close. | Handheld selfie angle of [img 1] looking into the lens, arm extended. Casual vlog style, snowy mountains in the immediate background. |
| **Ground Level** | Camera sits directly on the floor/surface, often with shallow depth of field. | Immersive close-up of the ground or environment. | Ground level shot, camera resting on the snow. Shallow depth of field with snow crystals in foreground, [img 1] walking in the background. |

## Part 3: Movement — Basic Moves

Fundamental camera motions that change the frame without changing camera position (except pedestal).

| Movement | Mechanic | Purpose | Example Prompt |
|----------|----------|---------|----------------|
| **Static** | No movement. The camera is locked off on a tripod. | Focus on subtle subject animation or internal conflict. | Static camera shot of [img 1]. She stands perfectly still while snow falls heavily around her and the wind moves her hair. Tripod shot. |
| **Pan Left / Pan Right** | The camera rotates horizontally on a fixed axis (like turning your head). | Follow a subject, reveal a new part of the scene. | Camera pans slowly from left to right, starting on a snowy tree and revealing [img 1] standing in the distance. |
| **Tilt Up / Tilt Down** | The camera rotates vertically on a fixed axis (like nodding your head). | Reveal a subject's height or a dramatic vertical structure. | Camera tilts up, starting at [img 1]'s boots in the snow and moving up to reveal her face and the mountain peak behind her. |
| **Zoom In** | The lens focal length changes to get closer. | Focus attention, flatten the background. | Slow zoom in on [img 1]'s face. The background compresses as the camera gets tighter on her expression. |
| **Zoom Out** | The lens widens to reveal more context. | Reveal context, shift focus to the environment. | Zoom out from [img 1]'s eye to reveal she is standing on the edge of a massive cliff. Establishing the environment. |
| **Pedestal Up / Down** | The *entire* camera physically moves up or down (elevator motion). | Change the subject's relationship to the ground or surrounding objects. | Camera pedestals up, rising from the snow level to eye-level with [img 1], revealing her stature against the mountains. |

## Part 4: Movement — Advanced Physical

Camera motions that involve physically moving the entire camera rig through space.

| Movement | Mechanic | Purpose | Example Prompt |
|----------|----------|---------|----------------|
| **Dolly In** | The camera physically moves *forward* toward the subject. | Increases intensity/intimacy, draws attention to a realization. | Smooth cinematic dolly in towards [img 1]. The camera physically pushes forward through the snow, getting closer to her. |
| **Dolly Out** | The camera physically moves *backward*. | Signals "leaving" or "conclusion," reveals the scale of the environment. | Cinematic dolly out from [img 1]. The camera physically moves backward, starting close to her face and retreating to show her alone. |
| **Truck Left / Truck Right** | The camera physically moves sideways (parallel to the subject). | Keeps pace with a moving subject, shows a dynamic journey. | Truck right camera movement. The camera slides sideways parallel to [img 1] as she stands in profile, revealing the army behind her |
| **Orbit / Arc** | The camera circles around the subject (360 degrees or partial). | Heroic and cinematic, shows the subject in their environment dynamically. | Camera orbits 360 degrees around [img 1]. She stands still in the center while the snowy background spins slowly behind her. |
| **Crane / Jib / Boom** | The camera sweeps up and over the scene from a high mechanical arm. | Grand reveals, establishing large environments from above. | Crane shot starting low on [img 1] and sweeping high up into the air, looking down at the snowy valley below |

## Part 5: Movement — Cinematic & AI

Specialized and stylized camera techniques—many of which are particularly effective in AI-generated motion content.

| Technique | Mechanic | Purpose | Example Prompt |
|-----------|----------|---------|----------------|
| **Dolly Zoom (The "Vertigo" Effect)** | The camera moves backward while zooming in (or vice versa). | The background warps while the subject stays the same size, creating disorientation. | Dolly zoom effect on [img 1]. The background mountains appear to warp and grow larger while she stays the same size. Vertigo effect |
| **Crash Zoom** | An incredibly fast, sudden zoom in. | Comedic or dramatic impact, shock effect. | Sudden crash zoom into [img 1]'s face. The camera snaps from a wide shot to an extreme close-up instantly. Dramatic impact. |
| **FPV Drone** | Fast, flying movement that banks, dives, and weaves through a space. | High energy, dynamic, immersive sense of speed. | Fast FPV drone shot flying through a snowy canyon and swooping past [img 1]. High speed, dynamic motion blur. |
| **Bullet Time** | Time freezes (or slows down) while the camera moves around the subject (Matrix style). | Highlights a critical, slowed moment in time, dynamic perspective. | Bullet time effect. Time is frozen with snowflakes suspended in mid-air, while the camera smoothly rotates 180 degrees around [img 1] |
| **Handheld** | Adds artificial "shake" to mimic a human operator. | Gritty and realistic, adds immediacy and urgency. | Handheld camera movement following [img 1]. The image has slight shake and organic motion, documentary war reporter style. |
| **Roll** | The camera rotates its horizon line (spinning the world upside down). | Creates extreme disorientation or visual flair. | Camera roll. The horizon line spins 360 degrees while focused on [img 1], turning the snowy world upside down and back again |
| **Rack Focus** | The focus shifts from a foreground object to a background object (or vice versa) without the camera moving. | Directs the viewer's attention, reveals a new object/person. | Rack focus shot. Starts focused on a sword in the foreground, then shifts focus to [img 1]'s face in the background. |
| **Pull Back Reveal** | Starts close on a detail and pulls back to show a surprising environment. | Dramatic reveal of context or scale. | Pull back reveal. Camera starts on the detail of [img 1]'s eye, then pulls back rapidly to show she is standing on top of a burning village |
| **Fly Through** | The camera moves *through* a window, keyhole, or object to enter a new space. | Seamless transition between spaces, dynamic and immersive. | Camera flies through a gap in the trees and past falling snow to land on a medium shot of [img 1]. |

## Combination Examples

Combine techniques from different categories to create sophisticated cinematic language:

| Combo | Result |
|-------|--------|
| **Low Angle + Dolly In + Close-Up** | A powerful, intimidating character revelation. |
| **Overhead + Static + Wide Shot** | A god's-eye view of isolation; graphic and detached. |
| **Handheld + POV + Ground Level** | Gritty, immersive, first-person chaos. |
| **Dutch Angle + Crash Zoom + Rack Focus** | Maximum disorientation and shock. |
| **Bullet Time + Orbit + Macro Shot** | A frozen, hyper-detailed, 360-degree reveal of a critical object. |
| **FPV Drone + Pull Back Reveal + Extreme Wide Shot** | A high-energy entrance that expands to show overwhelming scale. |

## Prompt Template

```
[Shot Type] of [Subject]. [Angle/Movement details]. [Environment description]. [Lighting/Atmosphere]. [Style/Genre keywords].
```

**Example:**
> *Low angle dolly in to a medium close-up of a cyberpunk mercenary. She towers over the camera, rain reflecting off her chrome arm. Neon-lit alleyway, volumetric fog, cinematic color grading, 35mm film grain, Blade Runner aesthetic.*
