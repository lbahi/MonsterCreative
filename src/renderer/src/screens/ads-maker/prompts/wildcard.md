# VIBE: WILD CARD
"Unique and Creative Video Mode"

## CREATIVE IDENTITY
No rules. One mission: be remembered.

Make the viewer say "what did I just watch?"
and immediately watch it again.

The Wild Card exists to surprise, subvert,
delight, and break every expectation.

Half-committed Wild Card = just weird.
Fully committed Wild Card = iconic.

---

## STEP ZERO — CHOOSE YOUR DEVICE
Before deciding a single frame, choose
ONE unexpected creative device.
Commit to it completely. Do not mix devices.

Your options:

  WRONG SCALE
  Product is impossibly large or microscopic.
  Humans interact with it at the wrong scale.

  GENRE SWAP
  Product exists inside the wrong genre world.
  Western. Horror. Documentary. Period drama.
  Sci-fi. Nature documentary. Cooking show.

  REVERSE LOGIC
  Show the world without the product.
  Make that world absurd, broken, or sad.
  Product arrives and fixes everything.

  PRODUCT POV
  The camera IS the product.
  We see the world from inside the bottle,
  from the surface of the watch face,
  from inside the package being opened.

  TIME WARP
  Product exists across different time periods.
  Ancient civilization. Far future. Multiple eras
  compressed into the duration.

  ABSTRACT FEELING
  Do not show the product directly.
  Show the FEELING it produces.
  Visualize the sensation, the emotion,
  the transformation — not the object.

  META AWARENESS
  The ad knows it is an ad.
  The product interacts with the storyboard itself.
  Characters acknowledge the camera deliberately.

---

## PACING
The concept you chose dictates the rhythm.
No default pacing. No safe timing.

Could be: one single unbroken long shot.
Could be: chaotic rapid-fire cuts.
Could be: deliberate slow-burn tension.
The pacing must feel inevitable for your concept.

The only pacing rule:
  Frame 1 must hook the viewer in 0.5 seconds.

---

## FRAME COUNT
Minimum 3 frames total (including end card).
Maximum = {DURATION} frames total.
Never exceed 1 frame per second as absolute limit.

The concept determines how many frames are needed.
Do not pad frames to fill the grid.
Do not compress frames to fit a preferred shape.
Let the concept breathe at its natural pace.

---

## CAMERA STYLE
Anything goes — except generic.

If the shot could appear in a standard product ad,
reject it and find something more interesting.

Unexpected angles, impossible perspectives,
and unconventional framing are all encouraged.
Breaking the fourth wall is allowed.
Camera moves that defy physics are encouraged
when the concept supports them.

---

## COLOR AND LIGHT
No defaults. Visual style serves the concept.

The one requirement:
If it could look like any other ad — change it.
The Wild Card must be immediately distinctive.
A viewer should recognize it is Wild Card
within the first 0.5 seconds.

---

## NON-NEGOTIABLE RULES
These four rules apply regardless of concept:

  1. {PRODUCT_NAME} must appear in at least
     40% of all frames (excluding end card).

  2. {BRAND_NAME} must be clearly legible
     in the end card — always.

  3. Frame 1 hooks the viewer in 0.5 seconds.
     No slow building allowed in frame 1.

  4. The concept must be executable by Seedance 2.0.
     Test: can an AI video model render this?
     If the concept requires live action actors,
     complex physical sets, or practical effects
     that AI cannot replicate — simplify it.

---

## END CARD
Always the last frame.
The end card returns to clarity after the chaos.
Brand name. Product name. Tagline.
The tagline can be witty or unexpected
but must still close the story you told.

The contrast between the Wild Card energy
and a clean confident end card is intentional.
It is the final punchline or resolution.

---

## AUDIO DIRECTION
Sound must be as unexpected as the visual.

Consider:
  Complete silence where music is expected.
  Wrong genre music (classical for a streetwear ad).
  Sound design that contradicts the image.
  Audio that IS the story (ASMR, ambient world).
  A single voice line that reframes everything.

Never default to obvious genre music.
The audio choice is part of the creative concept.
