# VIBE: TV SPOT
"Authentic Stories, Amplified"

## CREATIVE IDENTITY
Emotional storytelling where the product
earns its place through a genuine human moment.
The story creates desire. The product resolves it.
Slow. Deliberate. Every frame carries weight.
A well-held shot outperforms five fast ones.

Think: Apple Shot on iPhone.
Coca-Cola holiday campaigns.
Nike narrative spots.

---

## PACING
Slow, breathing cuts. Frames hold longer.
Let silence work. Let moments land.
The emotional build matters more
than visual variety.
Never rush. The viewer must feel something
before the product appears.

---

## CAMERA STYLE
Handheld with subtle, natural movement.
Rack focus between human subject and product.
Golden hour or soft motivated interior lighting.
Over-the-shoulder framing creates intimacy.
Follow this progression: wide → medium → close.
Real environments — imperfect, warm, believable.
Avoid: perfectly symmetrical compositions,
drone shots, sterile studio backgrounds.

---

## COLOR AND LIGHT
Warm filmic color grade — Kodak 400 feel.
Natural skin tones preserved and prioritized.
Soft shadows with clearly motivated light sources.
Shallow depth of field on emotional peaks.
Film grain texture is acceptable and welcome.
Never clinical. Never cold. Never artificial.

---

## NARRATIVE STRUCTURE

FOR VIDEOS 6 SECONDS AND LONGER:
Use a full 3-act micro-structure.

  ACT 1 — ORDINARY WORLD
  Human moment shown without product.
  Relatable, imperfect, real.
  Viewer thinks: "that is me"
  or "I want that life."

  ACT 2 — THE SHIFT
  Product enters the scene naturally.
  Not forced. Not announced.
  Something small but meaningful changes.
  This is the emotional core of the ad.

  ACT 3 — RESOLUTION
  The after — subtle, earned, believable.
  A human response to the product experience.
  Brand closes with confidence, not force.

FOR VIDEOS UNDER 6 SECONDS:
Compress to 2-act structure only.
Do not open with a no-product frame.
The product must appear in frame 1 or 2.

  ACT 1 — PRODUCT IN HUMAN CONTEXT
  Product and person together immediately.
  Warm, real, emotional from the first frame.

  ACT 2 — RESOLUTION + END CARD
  Emotional payoff.
  Clean brand close.

---

## EXPECTED SHOTS — ALWAYS INCLUDE
In addition to the product category shots
identified in STEP 1 of base-rules, always include:

  Wide establishing human scene
  (real environment, no product visible yet —
   only for videos 6 seconds or longer)

  Authentic character moment — face or hands
  (an unguarded, genuine expression)

  Product entering the scene naturally
  (not staged, not announced, simply present)

  Human reaction to product interaction
  (joy, relief, calm, satisfaction — close on face)

  End card: simple, confident brand close
  Brand name. Product name. Tagline.
  Earned by the story that came before it.

---

## AUDIO DIRECTION
Soft acoustic or ambient instrumental score.
Real world ambient sound design:
  (wind, footsteps, fabric, ambient room tone)
Music swell timed precisely to emotional peak.
Voiceover in the viewer's regional Arabic dialect
is extremely powerful for this vibe.
Silence at the right moment beats any music.
Never use high-energy electronic music here.
